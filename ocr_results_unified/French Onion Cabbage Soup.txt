RECIPE FOR: FRENCH ONION CABBAGE SOUP

2 TBS butter                    4 cups vegetable broth
1 small head cabbage            6 slices day-old, dark
cored & coarsely chopped        bread - rye or pumpernickel
2 yellow onions, chopped        1/4 lb medium-dry cheese,
1 tsp. salt                     such as Cantal, Edam,
2 tsp. black pepper             or Gruyere, grated

Preheat oven to 375Â°F.

1) In a large skillet over medium heat, melt 1/2 TBS butter. Add cabbage & onions & salt & pepper. Reduce heat to med.-low, cover, & cook until cabbage has wilted & reduced in volume, 5 or 6 minutes. Stir & continue to cook another 5 or 6 min.

2) In a large saucepan or soup pot, bring broth to a boil. Transfer cabbage & onions, along with cooking juices to boiling broth. Reduce heat to med.-low & simmer, uncovered, for 30 minutes.

3) Drain cabbage & onions, reserving the broth. Ladle half of the cabbage & onions into a large, deep oven-proof casserole dish. Arrange 4 bread slices on top & strew with 3/4 of the cheese. Ladle 1 1/2 cups of reserved broth into casserole. Add remaining cabbage & onions on top & ladle remaining broth.

4) Top w/ remaining bread & cheese & dot w/ remaining butter. Bake 'til cheese has melted & crust is golden, about 15 min.

PREPARATION TIME: TOTAL 1 HR 10 MINS SERVES: A LOT
